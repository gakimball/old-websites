Ice Hockey
Platform: NES
Release: 1988



I liked: the fast-paced action, cartoonish graphics, excellent music and animation
I didn't like: the goalie controls (but you get used to them)

I'm not really a big fan of hockey games, or even sports games in general. However, I've been much more drawn to 8-bit sports games such as Tennis, Tecmo Bowl, and Ice Hockey. There's NHL 07 and NHL 2k7 and all that good stuff, but Ice Hockey makes for a good, 8-bit, arcade-style game of hockey.

The premise of the game is still ice hockey: you command a four-man team of players, shootin' the puck into the goal and ice hockey stuff like that. But what makes this game unique is it's simple-but-deep team customization system.

You have four men on your team; each can be one of three body types, skinny, average, or heavy (aka fat). Each of the three builds has specific strengths and weaknesses: skinny players are very fast and maneuverable, although their shots are weaker. Heavy / fat players are slow powerhouses. Average players are in between, with average speed and shooting.

The default configuration is Fat / Avg / Avg / Skinny (which works pretty well), but since you can change around your lineup of players, it gives the game a surprising amount of depth and replay value. I've actually found that the "four fatties" make for a pretty good team. The large players absolutely slaughter the defense with their mad checking skills, and they've got mad shooting skills to boot. That's not to say the other two types are just straight up useless, though.

The game itself is basically... hockey. A very FAST game of hockey. Very fast. When I first picked up the game, it took me a while to get used to the pacing of the game. You control one player at a time, and can switch between them if the opposing team is on offense. When you hit the switching button your control shifts to whoever is closest to the puck, which isn't always clear. You get used to the pacing of the game after a while though, and you realize it couldn't be played any slower.

The control itself is very tight. Two buttons is all you need for this game, on offense or defense. On offense there's just shoot and pass, and on defense a button to switch players and one to steal the puck. You basically mash on the A button to steal the puck (if you have a human opponent, they should be too). If the skirmish lasts for more than a couple second, a field-wide fight breaks out, with the other six players on both teams jumping into your fight. A little comic "fight cloud" forms around them until the ref jumps in and breaks up the fight. Then someone is sent to the penalty box for a while.

Controlling the goalie is one of the more unorthadox parts of the game. The goalie is basically always in your control, moving around his little box. He's only controlled with the D-Pad, but since you command him and your own player while on defense, you have to shift your priorities to either actively controlling the goalie to stop a puck, or actively controlling a defensive player to steal the puck and shift to offense.

The game can be played in one- or two-player games of three quarters, seven, ten, or fifteen minutes each. The one player mode is pretty good, with a fairly challenging AI, albeit an AI you learn to trounce easily after about your third or fourth game. But the 2-player mode is an absolute blast, and definetly one of the best multiplayer experiences on the NES, if not just one of the best simultaneous multiplayer sports games for the system. In short, it's a lot of fun to play with a friend.

Graphically, the game holds up well. The players animate well, although they're presented in a much more cartoony, comical style than some gritty "realstic" 8-bit graphics, like NES games used to try and have back in the day.

The games soundtrack consists of four main tunes, one of which you memorize quickly, which is the game's main theme. One song plays continuously during gameplay, although it's not one that every gets old or annoying or anything. The sound effects are your basic skating, shooting, fighting, referee-whistling kind of stuff, which is great.

In short, if you're into NES sports, you'll definetly like this game. This coming from someone who isn't really much into sports games in the least.
